4. Construct a \(2 \times 2\) matrix, \(A = [a_{ij}]\), whose elements are given by: (i) \(a_{ij} = \frac{(i+j)^2}{2}\) (ii) \(a_{ij} = \frac{i}{j}\) (iii) \(a_{ij} = \frac{(i+2j)^2}{2}\)

A \(2 \times 2\) matrix A is of the form: $$A = \begin{pmatrix} a_{11} & a_{12} \\ a_{21} & a_{22} \end{pmatrix}$$

(i) \(a_{ij} = \frac{(i+j)^2}{2}\) $$a_{11} = \frac{(1+1)^2}{2} = 2$$ $$a_{12} = \frac{(1+2)^2}{2} = \frac{9}{2}$$ $$a_{21} = \frac{(2+1)^2}{2} = \frac{9}{2}$$ $$a_{22} = \frac{(2+2)^2}{2} = 8$$ So, $$A = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & \frac{9}{2} \\ \frac{9}{2} & 8 \end{pmatrix}$$

(ii) \(a_{ij} = \frac{i}{j}\) $$a_{11} = \frac{1}{1} = 1$$ $$a_{12} = \frac{1}{2}$$ $$a_{21} = \frac{2}{1} = 2$$ $$a_{22} = \frac{2}{2} = 1$$ So, $$A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & \frac{1}{2} \\ 2 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$$

(iii) \(a_{ij} = \frac{(i+2j)^2}{2}\) $$a_{11} = \frac{(1+2(1))^2}{2} = \frac{9}{2}$$ $$a_{12} = \frac{(1+2(2))^2}{2} = \frac{25}{2}$$ $$a_{21} = \frac{(2+2(1))^2}{2} = 8$$ $$a_{22} = \frac{(2+2(2))^2}{2} = 18$$ So, $$A = \begin{pmatrix} \frac{9}{2} & \frac{25}{2} \\ 8 & 18 \end{pmatrix}$$

Comparing the elements: \(a-b = -1\) (1) \(2a+c = 5\) (2) \(2a-b = 0\) (3) \(3c+d = 13\) (4)

Substitute \(a=1\) into (2): \(2(1)+c = 5 \implies 2+c = 5 \implies c = 3\).

Substitute \(c=3\) into (4): \(3(3)+d = 13 \implies 9+d = 13 \implies d = 4\).

So, \(a=1, b=2, c=3, d=4\).
