Action Plan: "PLUTO" Trailer - Cut 1 (Cosmic Mystery) - 90 Seconds

Objective: Build a slow-burn sense of awe and mystery that crescendos into cosmic dread, hooking the audience with a profound existential question.

Key Emotional Arc: Hope -> Curiosity -> Disbelief -> Revelation -> Terror

Cut 1: The Promise of Discovery (0-15 seconds)

1. A crew member in zero-g catches a floating sphere of water.

Cut 2: The Anomaly (15-40 seconds)

Timestamp Visuals (Video) Audio (Music, SFX, Dialogue/VO) On-Screen Text/Graphics
15-20s Cut: The Odyssey is now in low orbit. We see the iconic "Sputnik Planitia" (the heart), but it's streaked with faint, deep-red lines that seem to glow with a subtle internal light. Music: Piano continues, but a low, vibrating electronic hum begins to fade in underneath it, creating dissonance.
20-24s Cut: Close-up on a ship's console. The red lines are being mapped in 3D. They branch and connect like a vascular system. Dialogue (Tech #1, over comms): "Commander... are you seeing these energy readings? They're not geological." Text on Screen: ENERGY SIGNATURE: NON-GEOLOGICAL. BIO-THERMAL.
24-29s Cut: A remote probe descends to the surface. A robotic arm scrapes the red ice and collects a sample. The sample is a viscous, dark-red liquid. SFX: Mechanical whirring of the arm. A soft crackle as it breaks the ice.
29-35s Cut: In the ship's lab, a vial of the red fluid spins. DR. ARIS (30s, brilliant, intense) stares at her monitor, her face slowly draining of color. Her eyes are wide. VO (Commander Reid, losing her calm tone): "We thought it was ice. We thought it was rock... We were wrong."
35-40s Cut: Extreme close-up on Dr. Aris's trembling lips. Dialogue (Dr. Aris, whispering, filled with disbelief): "No... No, it's impossible." Music: The piano cuts out completely. Only the low, ominous hum remains, growing slightly in volume.

Cut 3: The Terrible Revelation (40-60 seconds)

Timestamp Visuals (Video) Audio (Music, SFX, Dialogue/VO) On-Screen Text/Graphics
40-45s Cut: Commander Reid stands behind Dr. Aris, looking at the main screen. The results are displayed in stark, clinical text. Dialogue (Commander Reid, voice strained): "Aris... what is it?" Text on Screen: SAMPLE ANALYSIS:
- COMPOSITION: PLASMA
- KEY PROTEINS: ALBUMIN, FIBRINOGEN
- MOLECULE: HEMOGLOBIN
45-48s Cut: A flash of a stunning CGI simulation: A younger, warmer Sun. A lush, blue-green Pluto orbits peacefully in the habitable zone. VO (Commander Reid, full of dawning dread): "This wasn't just a planet..." Music: A deep, resonant cello note joins the hum.
48-52s Cut: The simulation continues. The massive form of Jupiter sweeps past, its gravity tearing Pluto from its orbit and flinging it violently into the outer solar system. Dialogue (Analyst, panicked over comms): "Orbital dynamics confirm it! Pluto was pushed!" SFX: A low, cosmic "boom" as Jupiter passes.
52-56s Cut: Back in the lab. A new screen shows a DNA helix extracted from the plasma sample. Beside it, a reference helix for Homo sapiens. They spin and align perfectly. A 100% match. VO (Commander Reid, now a horrified whisper): "It was a cradle. Our cradle."
56-60s Cut: A wide shot of Pluto from the ship. The red "veins" across its surface begin to pulse slowly with a soft, crimson light. Once. Twice. Music: The hum intensifies. A low, rhythmic thump... thump... begins, like a colossal heartbeat.
