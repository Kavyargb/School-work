Notice Writing

Format

Name of the organization NOTICE Date Title Content - whom, what, details. Name Designation

Important Tips

  • Adhere to the specified word limit of 50 words.
  • Write the word NOTICE at the top.
  • Name and Place of the organization or issuing office should be mentioned.
  • Give an appropriate heading.
  • Write the date of issuing the notice.
  • Clearly mention the target group.
  • Purpose of the notice must be clear.
  • Mention all the relevant details (Date, Time, Venue).
  • Name and designation of the person issuing the notice.
  • Put the notice in a box.

Article Writing

Important Points

  • Article writing requires high degree of creativity, wide range of vocabulary and thorough knowledge of the subject.
  • It is a structured piece of writing to inform, to persuade, to entertain or to convince the reader.
  • Usually written in third person.
  • Is written in several paragraphs.
  • Language to be simple and grammatically correct.

Elements of an Article

  • Title or Heading - Should be thought-provoking and catchy, highlighting the key...
  • By line - Name of the writer.
  • Introduction - The first few sentences in the first para should introduce the topic and its main idea.
  • Description of the topic - The middle para should highlight the main facts of the topic like causes and effects, advantages & disadvantages etc.
  • Conclusion - Should conclude with your views on the topic or with a desire, and appeal or a warning.

The media shows things which range from interesting to questionable. One of the ways in which they project beauty is through women. Write an article on how media influence the life of women, specially teenage girls in around 200 words. You are Rahul / Rahani.
Media Influence On Teens

- By Rahul

The media has a strong impact on shaping people's thoughts and beliefs, especially when it comes to beauty and appearance. From television shows to social media platforms, women are often portrayed in ways that suggest how they 'should' look or behave. Beauty is shown as being only about slim bodies, flawless skin & expensive fashion.

Teenage girls, who are still discovering themselves, are the most affected by these media images. At a young age, they may begin comparing themselves to models and influencers they see online. This often leads to low self-confidence, body image issues, and even mental health struggles. Many feel pressured to look perfect, just to feel accepted in school or social circles.

Social media makes this pressure worse. With filters, likes, and followers, teens may start believing their worth depends on their looks or popularity. Some may try harmful beauty routines, strict diets etc. Only a few media campaigns promote body positivity.

In conclusion, the media must take responsibility for the messages it spreads. By showing real, diverse and unfiltered women, it can help teenage girls grow with confidence and self-love. True beauty should not be measured by trends but by being proud of who you are.

Invitation Writing

Types of Invitations

Invitations can be broadly classified into two types:

  • FORMAL: Used for official purposes or large gatherings.
    • Card Type: For inviting many people (e.g., school annual day, weddings). Written in the third person.
    • Letter Type: A formal letter to invite a specific individual, like a chief guest.
  • INFORMAL: Used for personal events with a few guests (e.g., birthday party, success party). Written in the first person, like a friendly letter.

Reply Writing

Replying to Invitations

Replies follow the tone of the invitation. A formal invitation requires a formal reply, and an informal invitation requires an informal reply.

  • Formal Replies: Written in the third person. Do not include a salutation or complimentary close. State acceptance or refusal clearly. If refusing, provide a brief reason.
  • Informal Replies: Written in the first person, like a personal letter. Use a friendly tone.
Your friend PV Satish has invited you to attend the wedding of his sister Jaya. You find that you have an important Pre-Board exam on the day of the wedding. Thus, you cannot attend the event.
